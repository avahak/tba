Facing a seemingly impossible situation, where he needed to sink the 5-ball while leaving the cue ball behind the 6-ball, Reyes devised a clever strategy. He intentionally banked the 5-ball off the foot rail, aiming for it to hit the 6-ball and nudge it towards the head rail pocket. However, the 5-ball unexpectedly sank into the pocket, leaving Reyes with a challenging predicament. With the cue ball now behind the 6-ball and no direct shot to the 6-ball, Reyes had to employ a creative solution. He carefully positioned the cue ball and executed a series of bank shots, zig-zagging the cue ball around the 6-ball and into the pocket. The shot defied expectations, demonstrating Reyes's exceptional cue ball control and ability to think outside the box. The Z-shot quickly gained fame, not only for its remarkable execution but also for the dramatic circumstances under which it was performed. It became a signature move for Reyes, showcasing his mastery of the game and his ability to rise to the occasion. The Z-shot has been analyzed and admired by pool enthusiasts worldwide, solidifying its place among the most iconic shots in pool history.
